Subhas Chandra Bose

Netaji

Here's a time line of Netaji's life:

  • 1897 - Cuttack, British India (now in Cuttack district, Odisha, India)
  • 1902 - Subhas entered the Baptist Mission's Protestant European School in Cuttack in January
  • 1912 - he secured the second position in the matriculation examination conducted under the auspices of the University of Calcutta
  • 1920 - Subhas Bose took the open competitive exam for them in August 1920 and was placed fourth
  • 1922 - Bose founded the newspaper Swaraj and assumed charge of the publicity for the Bengal Provincial Congress Committee. His mentor was Chittaranjan Das, a voice for aggressive nationalism in Bengal.
  • 1923 - Bose was elected the President of Indian Youth Congress and also the Secretary of the Bengal State Congress. He became the editor of the newspaper "Forward", which had been founded by Chittaranjan Das.
  • 1938 - Bose had become a leader of national stature and agreed to accept nomination as Congress President.
  • 1941 - Bose began his journey to reach Russia through British India's North West frontier with Afghanistan.
  • 1943 - after being disillusioned that Germany could be of any help in gaining India's independence, Bose left for Japan.
  • 1944 - in a speech broadcast by the Azad Hind Radio from Singapore, Bose addressed Mahatma Gandhi as the "Father of the Nation" and asked for his blessings and good wishes for the war he was fighting.
  • 1945 - Subhas Chandra Bose's death occurred from third-degree burns on 18 August 1945 after his overloaded Japanese plane crashed in Japanese-ruled Formosa (now Taiwan).
